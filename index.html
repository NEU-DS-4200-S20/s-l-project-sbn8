Dan Krasnonosenkikh, Christian Yiu, Jacob Hoffman, Tian Xia

Service-Learning Course Project as part of DS 4200: Information Presentation and Visualization, taught by Prof. Dylan Cashman, Data Visualization @ Khoury, Northeastern University.

Motivation

The following visualizations were created in collaboration with SBN in order help show off what SBN has been able to accomplish so far to potential sponsors. It is important for SBN to of acquire funding from organizations other than the State of Massachusetts in order for their organization to be more financially flexible. Our hope is that our visualization will be able to further highlight the successes of the food festival, especially for the further advancement of their mission statement.

One of the main things that SBN emphasizes is their diversity in their demographics. It is also a key feature that they are proud of wish to show off. As a result, our main focus will be to navigate the user through the our visualization to highlight their diversity.

Data Analysis

Summary of data, data types, and data preprocessing.

Expectation: Data sources match the problem statement and are appropriate. These descriptions should be very explicit so someone could read your page and properly reproduce your results.

We heavily utilized the zip-code data to locate attendees, and to act as bins for multiple attendees from the same area. Additionally, we use the income level (categorical), race (categorical), and favorite experiences (activity/cuisines) from the food festival (qualitative, non-categorical). The data is sourced from surveys handed out during the food festival and was then given to us in an excel file. Some preparation/preprocessing in excel was required to make managing the data go smoothly.

    Data Preprocessing Steps:
  1. Normalization of columns that exhibited a One-to-Many relationship
    • Several columns contained data which violated First Normal Form (1NF), which states that each table cell should only have one discrete piece of data
    • Solution was to create new tables for each column that displayed this One-to-Many relationship and have those tables reference each attendee ID (foreign key).
  2. Addition of longitude and latitude data
    • In addition to the zip-code data that was provided for each attendee, we also added the corresponding longitude and latitude coordinates for each zip-code in order to gain a more accurate location mapping in our final vizualization.
    • There are numerous online tools available for mapping US zip-codes to specific geographic coordinates, the addition of the longitude and latitude columns was done manually with the help of these tools.

Task Analysis

Summary of task table.

Expectation: Clearly describes domain tasks, processes, goals and abstract tasks for domain problems.

There are three main tasks that our vizualization hopes to accomplish.

  1. Demographic Analysis
    • Emphasizing the wide array of demographic backgrounds across all the food festival attendees is an important point of upsell to potential future sponsors. As a result, our vizualization should provide an interactive, user-friendly way to display the different categories of demographic information available to festival organizers (i.e. Race, Income levels, Attendee point of origin, etc.)
  2. Cuisine Popularity Analysis
    • We also want to be able to vizualize the popularity of particular food cuisines that are present at the Boston Food Festival.
    • In conjunction with our Demographic Analysis, we hope to be able to gain some insight in how particular food cuisines rate among members of different demographic groups.
  3. Food Purchasing Habits Analysis
    • If the mission of the Boston Local Food Festival is to raise awareness about various local food options, one measure of success could be whether attendees actually end up purchasing food products that were previously unknown to them.
    • Analysing the cuisines of newly purchased products could point out newly rising food trends among different demographic groups and provide insight as to what types of vendors could perform better in future events.

Conclusion
